OUTCOME ANALYSIS

Error matrix

The initial overall accuracy of the classification method trained with R. We feed our model by 1000 points from which we used 700 points for training and 300 for testing our model. The overall accuracy is 0.69, we think it is reliable. The accuracy of the classification method can be calculated by summing the number of true positives and true negatives and dividing it by the total number of data points. In landslide assessments, an error matrix, also known as a confusion matrix or a contingency table, is commonly used to evaluate the accuracy of a classification method. It provides a detailed breakdown of the classification results by comparing the predicted classes with the actual classes for a set of data points.

Typically, an error matrix for landslide assessments includes the following components:

Actual Classes: This column represents the true or actual classes of the data points in the test set. In the context of landslide assessments, these classes could be labelled as "landslide" and "non-landslide" to indicate whether a point experienced a landslide event or not.

Predicted Classes: This row represents the classes predicted by the classification method for the corresponding data points in the test set. The predicted classes are typically generated based on the trained model or algorithm. Count/Number of Points: The entries in the cells of the error matrix represent the number of data points that fall into each category actual class and predicted class combination.

Population Exposure Assessment:

Population exposure assessment is a process that aims to quantify and analyse the potential exposure of human populations to various hazards or risks. It involves identifying and characterizing the spatial distribution of population and determining their proximity or vulnerability to specific hazards or stressors. Population exposure assessment plays a crucial role in disaster risk management, public health planning, and environmental impact assessment, the key steps and considerations involved in population exposure assessment are as follow: Population data, Hazard identification, Exposure indicators, Vulnerability assessment, Risk Analysis, Visualization and Communication and Mitigation and planning.

According to the chart, in our study area, although most people live in areas with low risk of landslides, there are still more than 10% of people living in areas with more than medium risk. That is why the necessary landslide prevention measures are necessary.

Error we met and the solution

There are some errors we encountered during the implementation of the model.

1. To ensure accurate calculations in the model, it is important to align the spatial extent and pixel size of the environmental factor layers. Having consistent extents for each layer is crucial to avoid potential errors and issues during the model calculation process.

2. The name of all layers or files containing the environmental factor must be the same with the name input as parameters in the R model, otherwise it will give error and the calculation process will stop.

3. We should set the null data value to 9999 to avoid the error causing the calculation stop.
